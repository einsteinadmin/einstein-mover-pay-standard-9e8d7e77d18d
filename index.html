The pay ladder

Six rungs. Same direction: up.

Working as a Part-Time Lead?

Part-Time Lead isn't a separate rung on the ladder — it's a pay arrangement for movers who lead some shifts but not all. Your pay flips between two rates depending on what you're doing that day:

Shifts where you're leading
$20.00/hr base + up to $2.00/hr bonus

You earn Jr. Lead pay — base + full performance bonus eligibility — for every hour you're leading the job.

Shifts where you're not leading
$19.00/hr flat

You earn Lead In Training pay — flat hourly, no performance bonus — for hours you're not in the lead seat.

So your weekly take-home depends on how much of your time is spent leading. Your branch manager tracks this and you can ask any time what your shift breakdown looks like.

The Lead Performance Matrix

Scroll horizontally inside the grid if needed. Tap outside or × to close.

How to read it: Each quarter you earn 0–10 points per metric. Multiply each by the metric's weight (treated as a decimal: 34 = .34). Add up the totals across all metrics = your Quarterly Score, on a 0–10 scale. A Quarterly Score of 8/10 means you earn 80% of your position's max bonus for the quarter. Same matrix at every Lead rung — Jr. Lead through Lead General.

⚠ Driver Score void clauses

  • At-fault accident over $500 in the quarter → your driver score bonus is void.
  • Traffic violation in the quarter → your driver score bonus is void.
  • 1st curb-check damage is excused — assuming you weren't driving recklessly (e.g., hitting the curb going >10 mph). This won't immediately pull your bonus.
  • 2nd curb-check damage if >$500 → loss of bonus.

Worked example

You're a Regular Lead with a max bonus of $2.25/hr. This quarter:

  • Your team's damages per move hour averaged $1.50 → that's 8 points × .34 = 2.72
  • Your driver score averaged 93 → 9 points × .33 = 2.97
  • You had 1 callout in the quarter → 10 points × .33 = 3.30

Quarterly Score = 8.99/10 → you earn 89.9% of your $2.25/hr max → $2.02/hr in performance bonus. On a 480-hour quarter, that's about $971 — paid one month after quarter close. On top of your $20.50/hr base. Plus tips.

What changed & why

The policy details behind the headline.

Lead In Training is now a flat $19 — no performance bonus

We dropped the $1.50 performance bonus on the entry-level role and rolled it into a flat $19/hr base. Two reasons. First — your floor is your floor. No chasing a performance bonus while you're still learning. Second — we recruit Lead-quality candidates into this seat, and they deserve a pay offer that's clear from day one. LIT is explicitly a transitional role; the target is 3–6 months in seat before you're promoted.

Hall of Fame: officially an honor now

HoF used to be a separate paid position above Lead General — though for the past 2 years, Hall of Famers and Lead Generals have made the same base pay anyway. We're cleaning up the structure to match. Hall of Fame officially becomes an honor (not a pay band): swag, selectable PTO days, plaques, truck naming, and possible one-time bonuses. Current Hall of Famers continue on the Lead General pay path. The recognition stays. The structure just matches what's already been happening.

Higher base AND higher bonus ceilings at the leads

Every Lead position — Jr., Regular, Senior, Master, and General — got a base raise of $1.50 to $2.50/hr. On top of that, max performance bonuses went up for Jr., Regular, Sr., and Master Lead. Lead General's bonus max trimmed slightly because the base bump there is bigger ($19.50 → $22.00). When you stack it all up, the top of the ladder went up more than the middle, but the middle didn't get left behind.

Why these three things together?

Three connected ideas: (1) a stronger floor at entry so we can attract better candidates, (2) a cleaner ladder by collapsing HoF into Lead General, and (3) meaningful raises at every rung so movement up the ladder is the engine of pay growth. The Mover Comp Philosophy doc lays out the underlying principles — comp grows with mastery, the company shares wins with movers, and the structure has to be honest and durable.

FAQ

Answers to the questions we get most.

When does the quarterly bonus get paid out?

One month after the quarter ends. We need that month to finalize damage numbers and (eventually) customer satisfaction data.

What if I leave the company after the quarter ends but before the payout date?

You still get paid. The bonus goes out via direct deposit on the normal payout date.

How does the bonus work if I'm part-time?

Same percentages, prorated by hours worked. The pay calculator above will show you exactly what a 20- or 30-hour week looks like at your rung.

What if I'm in training but my star isn't earned yet?

You earn the Lead In Training base — flat $19/hr, no performance bonus. Once you earn your star and step into the Jr. Lead role, you move to the Jr. Lead pay band — $20 base + up to $2/hr performance bonus.

I'm currently in the Hall of Fame. What happens to my pay?

For most Hall of Famers, your pay doesn't change — Hall of Famers and Lead Generals have already been on the same pay path for the past 2 years. The Hall of Fame designation continues as an honor: swag, selectable PTO days, plaques, truck naming, possible one-time bonuses. The plaque doesn't go anywhere. If you were grandfathered into the older Hall of Fame pay band, your branch manager will walk through exactly what this means for your numbers.

How do I know what tier (Low / Medium / High) I'm at?

Your branch manager has the tier breakdowns. You can ask any time — we'd rather you know.

What exceptions are made for call-outs or late arrivals?

For call-outs, only two reasons keep one from being logged against your performance bonus: bereavement, or workers' comp following an on-site injury. Everything else counts.

For late arrivals, there are no exceptions — but a 10-minute clock-in window is already built in before a late point is recorded.

Any exception outside those rules has to be approved by a regional manager. And if your attendance is approaching termination levels, exceptions may be granted on the termination side (like requiring a doctor's note to avoid losing your job) — but that call-out or late still affects your performance bonus for the quarter.

Are my driver score and attendance points tied to a specific quarter?

Yes. Your driver score for the quarter is based on your Samsara score during that quarter's date range. Same with call-out and late-arrival points — only what happened in that quarter counts toward that quarter's bonus. New quarter, fresh start.

Since this is a bonus, will I pay more in taxes on it?

Short answer: no. Your total taxes at the end of the year are the same whether your pay came as regular wages or as a bonus.

Long answer: Bonuses are typically withheld at 22% on the individual paycheck — that's an IRS estimate, not your actual tax rate. At year-end, your real federal tax bracket is determined by your total income (wages + bonuses combined) and your filing status (single, married filing jointly, etc.).

If your actual bracket is higher than 22%, not enough was withheld during the year and you could owe more on your tax return. If your bracket is lower than 22%, more was withheld than you owe, and you'll get that difference back as a refund. Either way, the right amount of tax gets paid — bonuses don't cost you more overall.
